# IGA Rate Analysis - Executive Summary
**I-4 Corridor Federal Detention Facilities Comparative Analysis**
*February 27, 2026*

## Overview

This analysis compares Intergovernmental Agreement (IGA) rates and terms across five county detention facilities in the I-4 corridor that contract with the U.S. Marshals Service (USMS) to house federal detainees.

## Key Findings

### 1. **Extreme Rate Disparities** ⚠️

**Per-Diem Rates:** Range from $40.00 to $101.06/day (152.7% variance)

| Facility | Rate | Years Since Update |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| Hillsborough County | $101.06 | 21 years (2005) |
| Orange County | $88.00 | 15 years (2011) |
| Pinellas County | $80.00 | Unknown |
| John E. Polk (Seminole) | $56.71 | 22 years (2004) |
| Osceola County | $40.00 | **40 years (1986)** ⚠️ |

**Guard Rates:** Range from $12.00 to $31.75/hour (164.6% variance)

### 2. **Osceola County Anomaly - CRITICAL** 🚨

- **40-year-old rate** with ZERO modifications since 1986
- Current rate: $40.00/day (lowest in corridor)
- If inflation-adjusted: Should be ~$103.64/day
- **Estimated county subsidy: $329,152/year** (taxpayers subsidizing federal detention)
- Questions: Why has this never been updated? Is facility still operational?

### 3. **Hillsborough County Pricing Concerns**

- **Highest per-diem rate** ($101.06) despite 21 years without review
- **Overcharge history:** 1996 modification required rate reduction for overcharges
- **202% increase** over 22 years (1983-2005), but no updates since
- Questions: Is current rate justified? Have there been more overcharges?

### 4. **Orange County - Unique ICE Authorization**

- **Only facility** explicitly authorized to house ICE detainees
- **Guard rate premium:** $31.75/hr (52% higher than Hillsborough)
- **114 total beds** (94 male, 20 female)
- Questions: Does ICE pay different rates? Are ICE/USMS detainees mixed?

### 5. **No Clear Methodology**

- No documented rationale for rate variations
- Pinellas has explicit 36-month adjustment pathway via DSNetwork
- Others have gone 15-40 years without updates
- Suggests inconsistent management across USMS district

## Conclusion

This analysis reveals **significant inequities** in federal detention pricing across the I-4 corridor. The 152.7% variance in per-diem rates, combined with 40-year-old agreements and documented overcharge history, suggests systemic issues in USMS rate management.

**The Osceola County anomaly is particularly concerning** and requires immediate investigation. A 40-year-old rate that has never been updated is unprecedented and raises questions about either county subsidization of federal detention or undocumented side agreements.

**Hillsborough County's highest-in-corridor rate**—combined with its overcharge history and 21-year stagnation—also warrants scrutiny to ensure taxpayers are not being overcharged.

**Immediate action is recommended** to request current billing data, verify operational status, and audit rate-setting methodologies.
