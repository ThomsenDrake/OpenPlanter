## Tips for FOIA Success

### 1. Submit to Multiple Agencies
- ICE, GSA, and DHS likely all have responsive records
- Cross-agency requests increase chances of getting information

### 2. Be Specific but Broad
- Include specific addresses and dates
- Use broad categories to avoid limiting the search

### 3. Request Fee Waiver
- News media, educational institutions, and researchers qualify
- Emphasize public interest and contribution to public understanding

### 4. Follow Up Aggressively
- Agencies have 20 business days to respond (often miss deadlines)
- Send follow-up emails every 2 weeks
- File appeals promptly if denied

### 5. Use FOIAonline
- Create account at https://foiaonline.gov
- Track all requests in one place
- Download responsive records immediately

### 6. Appeal Denials
- If denied, appeal within 90 days
- Cite specific exemptions agency used
- Argue why public interest outweighs exemptions

### 7. Coordinate with Journalists
- Share responsive records with journalists
- Encourage news outlets to file similar requests
- Publicize delays or denials

## Expected Timeline

**Initial Response:** 20 business days (statutory requirement)  
**Actual Response:** Often 2-6 months  
**Appeals:** 20 business days for appeal determination  

**Strategy:** Submit all requests simultaneously to maximize information flow

---

## What to Do with FOIA Responses

1. **Index and Catalog:** Create database of all responsive records
2. **Redaction Analysis:** Note what was withheld and why
3. **Cross-Reference:** Compare across multiple FOIA responses
4. **Publication:** Release to journalists, activists, community groups
5. **Further Requests:** Use information to refine next round of requests
