## Executive Summary

The Scout microtransit program is an on-demand, door-to-door ridesharing service in Seminole County, Florida, that replaced most fixed-route LYNX bus service beginning in October 2025. Operated by Freebee, LLC (d/b/a BeFree LLC), Scout was created in response to rising LYNX costs (from $7 million in 2019 to $17 million projected for 2025) and low ridership on fixed routes. The planning process began in summer 2024, with a competitive RFP process in winter 2024-2025, contract award in May 2025, and full service launch in October 2025.

**Key Findings:**
- **Initiation**: Board of County Commissioners directed staff in summer 2024 to explore alternatives to the LYNX fixed-route system
- **Cost Driver**: LYNX costs increased from $7M (2019) to $17M (2025), a 143% increase
- **Ridership Problem**: Some 40-foot buses averaged half a rider on Sundays
- **Vendor Selection**: Competitive RFP process with 3 vendors; BeFree LLC selected unanimously by commissioners
- **Timeline**: 16 months from initial direction to full launch (June 2024 - October 2025)
- **Operator**: Freebee, LLC, Miami-based microtransit company founded in 2012

## Decision Points and Rationale

### Why Replace LYNX?

**1. Cost Escalation**  
LYNX costs to Seminole County increased from $7 million (2019) to $17 million (2025), representing a 143% increase over 6 years.

*Source*: Commissioner Amy Lockhart, as cited in My Sanford Herald and News 13 (April/May 2025)

**2. Low Ridership**  
Some 40-foot buses averaged 0.5 riders on Sundays, indicating severe inefficiency.

*Source*: Assistant County Manager Kristian Swenson, as cited in NACo article (Feb 11, 2026)

**3. Fixed-Route Limitations**  
Fixed routes left many residents in "transit deserts" without service.

*Source*: Kristian Swenson, NACo article

**4. Opportunity for Innovation**  
Board wanted to explore more efficient, demand-responsive models.

*Source*: NACo article, meeting transcripts

---

### Why Microtransit?

**1. Flexibility**  
On-demand service can respond to actual rider needs rather than fixed schedules.

**2. Cost Efficiency**  
Smaller vehicles and shared rides reduce per-trip costs.

**3. Coverage**  
Door-to-door service reaches residents in transit deserts.

**4. Model Success**  
Arlington, Texas's boundless microtransit model demonstrated viability.

*Source*: NACo article, staff research

---

### Why Freebee?

**1. Competitive Pricing**  
BeFree LLC's proposal was within the competitive range ($4.8M-$9.2M among vendors).

**2. Experience**  
Freebee operates 150+ vehicles across 40+ communities, demonstrating operational capability.

**3. Technology Platform**  
Established app (Ride Freebee) and dispatch system ready for deployment.

**4. Electric Fleet**  
Commitment to electric vehicles aligned with county sustainability goals.

*Source*: Meeting transcripts, Freebee corporate profile

## Planning Methodology

### RFP Approach

The county intentionally issued a "purposely vague" RFP to let the market propose solutions without staff prejudicing parameters.

*Source*: Kristian Swenson, NACo article (Feb 11, 2026)

This approach allowed:
- Private sector creativity in service design
- Comparison of different operational models
- Negotiation of terms based on actual proposals

- Microtransit designed to complement, not fully replace LYNX

## Conclusions

### Summary of Creation and Planning

The Scout microtransit program emerged from a straightforward cost-benefit analysis by Seminole County commissioners facing unsustainable LYNX cost increases. The planning process was notably:

**Compressed**: 16 months from initial direction (summer 2024) to full launch (October 2025)

**Market-Driven**: RFP designed to let private sector propose solutions rather than prescribe a model

**Transparent**: April 2025 public meeting to discuss policy questions before final decisions

**Phased**: Soft launch in September 2025 allowed for testing before full deployment

---

### Key Success Factors

1. **Clear Cost Driver**: 143% LYNX cost increase made change imperative
2. **Model Availability**: Successful microtransit deployments elsewhere (Arlington, TX) provided template
3. **Experienced Vendor**: Freebee's existing operations in 40+ communities reduced startup risk
4. **Technology Ready**: Ride Freebee app already operational, reducing development time
5. **Political Consensus**: Unanimous commissioner support enabled rapid decision-making

---

### Outstanding Questions

1. **Long-Term Cost Comparison**: Will Scout actually cost less than LYNX over 5-10 years?
2. **Rider Satisfaction**: Are residents better served by door-to-door vs. fixed routes?
3. **Equity Impact**: Are transportation-disadvantaged residents adequately served?
4. **Scalability**: Can the model handle demand growth without cost escalation?
5. **Labor Standards**: What are driver wages and working conditions vs. LYNX union jobs?
