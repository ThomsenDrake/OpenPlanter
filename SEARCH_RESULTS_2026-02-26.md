## 3. Financial Dispute: Orange County IGSA Reimbursement

### 💰 **MAJOR ISSUE: Reimbursement Rate Shortfall**

**Current Rate:** $88/day  
**Actual Cost:** $180/day  
**Shortfall:** $92 per detainee per day (104% cost overrun)  
**Status:** Mayor Jerry Demings actively negotiating with USMS for higher rates  

**Timeline:**
- Orange County has housed 5,000+ ICE detainees in past 8 months
- Demings sent letter to USMS demanding renegotiation (December 2025)
- County extended deadline for resolution (February 13, 2026)
- Dispute ongoing

**Implications:**
- Financial pressure on county budgets
- May affect other IGSA agreements along corridor
- Could lead to capacity changes if counties refuse to continue at current rates

## 5. 287(g) Program: Statewide Compliance Achieved

### ✅ **ALL Florida County Jails Now Have 287(g) Agreements**

**Announcement:** Florida Sheriffs Association confirmed statewide compliance (2025)

## 6. Oversight Crisis: ICE Facility Inspections Plummeted

### ⚠️ **36.25% DROP IN INSPECTIONS DURING 2025**

**Key Findings:**
- ICE detention facility inspection reports dropped 36.25% in 2025 vs. 2024
- No facility received more than one inspection in 2025 (supposed to be twice per year)
- Detention population increased 78% (to 68,440 people as of mid-December 2025)
- 32 deaths in ICE custody in 2025 (record high since 2004, nearly 3x 2024)

**Implications for I-4 Corridor:**
- Reduced oversight of conditions at county jail IGSA facilities
- Potential for increased health and safety violations
- FOIA requests for inspection reports even more critical

**Source:** Project On Government Oversight - https://www.pogo.org/investigates/ice-inspections-plummeted-as-detentions-soared-in-2025

**Confidence:** Confirmed (data analysis by POGO and American University)
