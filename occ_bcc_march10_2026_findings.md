## FINDING 1: RESOLUTION OPPOSING ICE DETENTION FACILITY

### Agenda Item
- **Item Number:** D-1 (Consent Agenda)
- **Sponsor:** Commissioner Nicole Wilson (District 1)
- **Action Requested:** Approval of Resolution
- **Outcome:** **APPROVED UNANIMOUSLY**

### Resolution Details
The resolution formally opposes the location of a U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) processing or detention facility in warehouses within unincorporated Orange County.

**Key Provisions:**
- Opposes conversion of industrial warehouses to detention facilities
- Cites incompatibility with Orange County's "communities, values and priorities"
- Expresses concern about impacts on tourism, quality of life, and local resources (water, sewer, municipal services)
- Notes Orange County's status as a "globally recognized visitor destination"
- States that ICE detention facilities are "unsuitable" for the county

### Targeted Facility
- **Address:** 8660 Transport Drive, Orlando (near Lake Nona)
- **Size:** 439,945 square feet
- **Status:** ICE officials toured this warehouse in January 2026
- **Context:** Part of a nationwide federal effort to identify nearly two dozen warehouses for detention capacity expansion under the Trump administration's mass deportation agenda

### Official Statements

**Mayor Jerry Demings:**
> "Our responsibility is to protect the health, safety and well-being of our residents, preserve the integrity of our local land use process and ensure that major decisions affecting our community are not made without transparency, coordination or respect for local government."

> "Orange County is committed to public safety, to the rule of law and to treating every person with dignity, and this resolution makes clear that any proposal of this nature is incompatible with our communities, values and priorities."

**Commissioner Nicole Wilson (Resolution Champion):**
> "Orange County is both a residential community and a globally recognized visitor destination and the placement of a detention and processing facility would strain limited water, sewer and municipal services while harming neighborhood quality of life and the County's international visitor experience and reputation."

### Public Comment Highlights

**Meilyn Santana** (Hope CommUnity Center, Apopka):
> "The stress, fear, and constant uncertainty about whether families will be safe tomorrow are weighing heavily on our community. Parents are afraid, children are anxious, and entire families are living with a level of fear that no community should have to endure."

**Therese O'Hara** (Ocoee resident):
> "Building a modern-day 'Dachau Disney' in a region defined by family and magic will not bring prosperity — it will bring a permanent stain to our soul."

### Legal Limitations
- The resolution is **symbolic** — it cannot legally prevent federal officials from proceeding
- Florida state statutes (Chapter 908) require municipalities to provide "best efforts" to support federal immigration enforcement
- City and county attorneys have cited constitutional barriers to limiting federal immigration activities

## FINDING 2: IGSA (INTERGOVERNMENTAL SERVICES AGREEMENT) STATUS

### Background
Orange County operates under an Intergovernmental Services Agreement (IGSA) with the U.S. Marshals Service (USMS) to house federal inmates and ICE detainees at the Orange County Jail.

### Current Dispute

**Reimbursement Rate Gap:**
- **Current rate:** $88 per day per detainee
- **Problem:** Rate is below actual county costs
- **Result:** Orange County is losing money on federal detainees

**ICE Detainee Population Surge:**
- January 2026: ICE detainees booked without local criminal charges surged by a factor of four
- This surge has exacerbated the funding shortfall

### Mayor Demings' Actions (February 2026)

**February 5, 2026:** Announced cap on ICE detainees at county jail and halt to the practice of moving people in and out

**February 13, 2026:** Sent letter to U.S. Marshals Service threatening to terminate the IGSA if reimbursement rates aren't renegotiated

**February 20, 2026:** Extended deadline for federal officials to respond while maintaining pressure

**Legal Consideration:** County attorneys are exploring a lawsuit against the federal government for breach of the IGSA contract

### Status as of March 10, 2026 Meeting
- The IGSA remains in dispute
- No agenda item specifically addressed IGSA renegotiation at this meeting
- Mayor Demings continues to press for higher reimbursement rates
- County has implemented measures to reduce the jail's daily population of ICE detainees

### Related Context

**February 10, 2026 BCC Meeting:**
- Commissioners discussed the funding shortfall for ICE detainees
- The board considered action on ICE reimbursement issues
- Discussion included potential responses to the proposed ICE detention warehouse
