# Trump Administration ICE Operations: I-4 Corridor Analysis
**Analysis Date:** February 27, 2026  
**Period Covered:** January 2025 - February 2026 (14 months of Trump Administration)  
**Focus:** Current operational structure, weak points for pressure, and data gaps

---

## Executive Summary

The second Trump administration has dramatically escalated ICE operations in Florida's I-4 corridor through a combination of executive orders, massive funding increases, and rapid expansion of 287(g) agreements. **Florida's ICE detention population has grown 278% from March 2024 to February 2026**, reaching 5,231 detainees statewide. The I-4 corridor now represents a critical hub in this expanded enforcement infrastructure.

**Key Findings:**
- **3 active detention facilities** along I-4 corridor (up from 0 in FY2020)
- **8 facilities with 287(g) authority** (most signed in Feb-March 2025)
- **303 current detainees** in I-4 facilities (Feb 2026 TRAC data)
- **$75 billion federal funding** approved for ICE through 2029
- **1,427 287(g) agreements nationwide** (957% increase from Dec 2024)

**Critical Weak Point:** The **three-tier system** creates structural vulnerabilities where most 287(g) facilities operate as booking points without detention capacity, creating transfer dependencies that could be disrupted through targeted political pressure.

## Part 1: Current ICE Operations Under Trump (Jan 2025 - Feb 2026)

### A. Federal Policy Framework

#### Executive Orders (January 20, 2025)

**1. Executive Order 14159: "Protecting the American People Against Invasion"**
- **Mandate:** Authorizes 287(g) agreements "to the maximum extent permitted by law"
- **Impact:** Required ICE to expand state/local law enforcement partnerships
- **Result:** 957% increase in 287(g) agreements nationwide (from ~135 to 1,427)

**2. Border Emergency Declaration**
- **Mechanism:** Allowed ICE to bypass competitive contracting processes
- **Impact:** No-bid contracts to private prison companies (CoreCivic, GEO Group)
- **Financial:** $45 billion allocated for new detention centers

**3. Rescission of Protected Areas Policies**
- **Previous Policy:** ICE avoided arrests at schools, churches, hospitals
- **New Policy:** No location protections
- **Impact:** Expanded enforcement reach into previously protected spaces

#### Funding Transformation

| Funding Aspect | Pre-Trump (2024) | Trump Era (2025-2029) | Change |
|----------------|------------------|-----------------------|---------|
| **ICE Annual Budget** | ~$8 billion | $75 billion (through 2029) | +837% |
| **National Detention Population** | <40,000 | 70,000 (Feb 2026) | +75% |
| **Target Bed Capacity** | 40,000 | 170,000+ by Nov 2026 | +325% |
| **Florida Detention Population** | 1,385 (Mar 2024) | 5,231 (Feb 2026) | +278% |
| **Private Prison Revenue** | Baseline | +13% in 2025 ($2B+ each) | Record profits |

**Source:** Brennan Center for Justice, ICE.gov, TRAC Immigration
