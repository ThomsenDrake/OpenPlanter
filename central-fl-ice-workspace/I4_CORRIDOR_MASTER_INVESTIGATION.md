## Core Findings
- 9 facilities identified across 6 counties in the I-4 corridor.
- 3 facilities are confirmed in ICE detention statistics as active detention nodes.
- 8 facilities hold 287(g) authority, but most do not appear in ICE detention statistics.
- The operational pattern is best modeled as a three-tier system: active detention, 287(g)-only processing, and private-contractor anomaly.
- Major reimbursement and cost asymmetries indicate county-level fiscal pressure points.
- **PSL-Resources Integration (March 2026):** Orange County IGSA crisis reaches March 13, 2026 deadline; county has ended rebooking practice and capped detainees; 287(g) over-implementation documented across Florida.

## Legal and Operational Framework
- IGSA (direct ICE-county detention contracts) and USMS IGA pass-through contracting coexist in the corridor.
- 287(g) authorizes immigration enforcement activity but does not itself prove long-term detention capacity.
- TRAC and ICE official statistics can disagree on facility visibility and classification, requiring explicit contradiction tracking.

## BOA Mandate Investigation (March 2026)

**Investigation Date:** March 5, 2026  
**Full Report:** `BOA_MANDATE_INVESTIGATION.md`  
**Quick Reference:** `BOA_FACTS_BRIEF.md`  

### Question
Can County Commissions in Florida be forced into a BOA (Basic Ordering Agreement) with the federal government if they terminate an IGSA/287(g) agreement?

### Answer
**NO.** BOAs are 100% VOLUNTARY under federal law, constitutional precedent, and Florida state law.

### Key Legal Authorities

1. **Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR 16.703)** - BOAs are voluntary procurement instruments, not mandates
2. **Printz v. United States (1997)** - Supreme Court prohibits federal commandeering of local officials
3. **Florida SB 168** - Requires cooperation, not program participation

### Orange County Reality

**County Claim:** BOA is "mandatory" if IGSA terminated  
**Legal Reality:** County can terminate IGSA, refuse BOA, limit detention to 48-hour detainers

**Financial Truth:** County's "crisis" is a CHOICE. They lose $92/day voluntarily.

| Scenario | Revenue | Cost | Net |
|----------|---------|------|-----|
| Current IGSA | $88/day | $180/day | -$92 |
| BOA | $25/day | $180/day | -$155 |
| No IGSA, No BOA | $0 | $0 | $0 |
