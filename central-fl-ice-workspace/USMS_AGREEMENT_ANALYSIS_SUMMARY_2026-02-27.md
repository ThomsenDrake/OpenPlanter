## Executive Summary

This analysis extracted comprehensive financial data and facility information from the USMS Intergovernmental Agreement (IGA) documents for Orange County, Florida detention facilities. The documents span from the original 1983 agreement through 2022 modifications, revealing a three-facility system with varying per-diem rates and complex multi-agency usage.

### Key Findings

1. **Three distinct facility codes** operating under a single IGA agreement (18-04-0023)
2. **Per-diem rates ranging from $80.26 to $88.00** per detainee day
3. **Historical rate increase of 193%** from $30.00 (1983) to $88.00 (current)
4. **Multi-agency authorization** including USMS, BOP, and ICE
5. **Comprehensive billing infrastructure** with separate invoicing for each federal component

## Medical & Financial Terms

### Medical Care Responsibilities

| Care Type | Responsible Party | Billing |
|-----------|-------------------|---------|
| **Inside Facility** | Local Government | Included in per-diem |
| **Outside Facility** | Federal Government | Direct from provider to Feds |
| **Emergency Outside** | Federal Government | Immediate notification required |

### Medical Program Details

#### TB Testing Requirements
- **Timeline:** Within 14 days of intake
- **PPD Reading:** 48-72 hours
- **Documentation:** Medical record + USMS District within 30 days

#### National Managed Care Contract (NMCC)
- Facility encouraged to use NMCC Discount Pharmacy Program
- Applies to non-OTC medications
- Reduces costs through federal purchasing power

#### Co-Payment Policy
- **Federal detainees MAY be charged** medical co-payments (Title 18 USC 4013(d))
- **Federal Government NOT responsible** for co-payments
- **Indigent detainees** cannot be denied care for non-payment

### Rate Adjustment Mechanism

#### Eligibility
- **Timeline:** After 36 months of agreement
- **Frequency:** No more than once per year
- **Notice:** 60 days prior to desired effective date

#### Process
1. Submit request via DSNet
2. Provide supporting documentation
3. Federal evaluation against four criteria:
   - Independent government estimate (Core Rate)
   - Similar facility comparisons
   - Historical pricing analysis
   - Operating expense evaluation

#### Constraints
- **Inflation Cap:** Shall not exceed National Inflation Rate (U.S. Dept of Commerce) unless justified
- **New Rate Effectivity:** First day of month following approval
