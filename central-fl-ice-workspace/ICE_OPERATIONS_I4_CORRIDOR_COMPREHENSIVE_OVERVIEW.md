### The Central Mystery

**Only 3 of 9 I-4 corridor facilities appear in official ICE detention statistics**, despite 8 facilities having active 287(g) agreements authorizing immigration enforcement. This suggests a complex two-tier system where most 287(g) facilities operate as booking/identification points rather than long-term detention facilities.

## Legal Framework

ICE detention operations in the I-4 corridor operate under **three distinct legal mechanisms**:

### 1. Intergovernmental Agreements (IGA) - USMS Route

**Definition:** County contracts with U.S. Marshals Service (USMS) to house federal detainees, including ICE detainees as a "pass-through."

**Facilities Using This Model:**
- Orange County Jail
- Pinellas County Jail

**Characteristics:**
- Counties bill USMS, not ICE directly
- ICE detainees mixed with USMS prisoners
- Less transparent (ICE doesn't appear as direct contractor)
- Per-diem rates set through USMS negotiation

**Evidence:**
```
Source: ICE Detention Statistics FY2020-FY2026
Classification: "USMS IGA" for Orange and Pinellas facilities
```

### 2. Intergovernmental Service Agreements (IGSA) - Direct ICE Route

**Definition:** Direct contract between ICE and county facility.

**Facilities Using This Model:**
- Hillsborough County Orient Road Jail

**Characteristics:**
- Direct ICE-county relationship
- More transparent billing
- ICE appears as direct contractor

**Evidence:**
```
Source: ICE Detention Statistics FY2026
Classification: "IGSA" for Hillsborough County Jail
First appeared: FY2026 (October 2025)
```

### 3. 287(g) Program - Immigration Enforcement Authority

**Definition:** Delegation of immigration enforcement authority to local law enforcement officers.

**Facilities with 287(g) Agreements:**
- Hillsborough County (Orient Road & Falkenburg Road)
- Pinellas County
- Polk County
- Orange County
- Seminole County (John E. Polk)
- Volusia County (Branch Jail & Correctional Facility)

**Models Implemented:**
- **Task Force Model:** Deputized officers can interrogate and arrest non-citizens
- **Warrant Service Officer Model:** Officers serve ICE warrants within facility

**Critical Distinction:** 287(g) authorization ≠ ICE detention capability. Most 287(g) facilities do NOT appear in ICE detention statistics.

### 4. USMS Detention Agreements (Federal Prisoner Housing)

Separate from ICE, several counties have Intergovernmental Agreements with USMS to house federal prisoners (pre-trial, BOP transfers). These agreements set per-diem rates for all federal detainees:
