## INVESTIGATION OVERVIEW

### Objective
Identify all active and planned ICE (Immigration and Customs Enforcement) detention facilities along Florida's Interstate 4 corridor, from Tampa to Daytona Beach.

### Geographic Scope
- **Counties:** Hillsborough, Pinellas, Polk, Orange, Seminole, Volusia
- **Major Cities:** Tampa, Clearwater, Lakeland, Orlando, Sanford, Daytona Beach
- **Corridor Length:** ~140 miles

### Investigation Approach
Multi-source analysis combining:
- Official ICE documentation (287(g) MOAs, facility listings)
- County sheriff's office records
- Local news media (Tampa Bay Times, Orlando Sentinel, Spectrum News)
- Advocacy organization reports (TRAC Immigration, DetentionReports.com)
- Government contract databases

## IDENTIFIED LIMITATIONS & GAPS

### High-Priority Data Gaps
1. **Missing ICE Facility Codes** - Only 2 of 9 facilities have confirmed FICE codes
2. **Unknown Detainee Populations** - Exact ICE detainee counts not available for most facilities
3. **IGSA Contract Terms** - Start/end dates, renewal language, financial terms not publicly available
4. **ICE-Dedicated Capacity** - Most facilities report total capacity, not ICE-dedicated beds
5. **Orlando Facility Status** - Planned facility not officially announced by ICE

### Moderate-Priority Gaps
6. **Historical Data/Trends** - No longitudinal data on detainee populations
7. **Transfer Patterns** - Unknown where detainees are transferred after processing
8. **Real-Time Population** - No mechanism for current population tracking
9. **287(g) Operational Changes** - Recent policy changes not documented publicly
10. **Adjacent County Facilities** - Possible facilities in adjacent counties not investigated

## FOIA STRATEGY DEVELOPED

### Overall Assessment
**12 of 15 limitations (80%) can be addressed through FOIA and Florida Sunshine Law requests**

### Tier 1 - HIGH PRIORITY (Submit First)
1. **IGSA Contracts** - Submit to 6 County Sheriff's Offices via Florida Sunshine Law
   - Timeline: 10-30 days
   - Success Likelihood: 90%
   - Why: State law faster than federal FOIA, fewer exemptions

2. **ICE Facility Codes** - Submit to ICE ERO
   - Timeline: 20-30 days
   - Success Likelihood: 95%
   - Why: Administrative data, minimal exemption risk

3. **287(g) Program Records** - Submit to ICE 287(g) Office + County Sheriffs
   - Timeline: 10-45 days
   - Success Likelihood: 85-90%
   - Why: Public program, oversight documents routinely released

### Tier 2 - MODERATE PRIORITY (Submit After Tier 1)
4. **Historical Population Data** - ICE ERO + Office of Immigration Statistics
   - Timeline: 30-60 days
   - Success Likelihood: 70%

5. **Orlando Facility Procurement** - ICE Acquisition + GSA
   - Timeline: 30-45 days
   - Success Likelihood: 60%
   - Challenge: May claim "pre-decisional" exemption

6. **Transfer Pattern Data** - ICE ERO
   - Timeline: 30-60 days
   - Success Likelihood: 65%

7. **ICE-Dedicated Capacity** - ICE ERO + County Sheriffs
   - Timeline: 20-45 days
   - Success Likelihood: 75%

### Tier 3 - LOW PRIORITY
8. **Adjacent County Facilities** - Additional research + FOIA
   - Timeline: Varies
   - Success Likelihood: Varies

### Not FOIA-Able
9. **Real-Time Population Data** - FOIA not suitable for continuous monitoring
   - Alternative: County jail booking systems (some public)

## METHODOLOGY NOTES

### Source Triangulation
Each facility was cross-referenced across:
- Minimum 2 independent sources for "Confirmed" status
- Official government documents (ICE MOAs, county records) prioritized
- News reports used as supporting evidence only
- Advocacy organization data cross-checked against official sources

### Data Quality Standards
- **Address Verification:** All addresses confirmed via multiple sources
- **Capacity Verification:** Cross-checked against facility websites, news reports, and government records
- **Contract Dates:** Only recorded when appearing in official documents
- **ICE Facility Codes:** Only recorded when confirmed in official databases

### Known Limitations
1. **Information Currency:** Data reflects publicly available information as of February 20, 2026
2. **Planned Facilities:** Orlando ICE Processing Center not officially announced by ICE
3. **Contract Transparency:** Private facility contracts have limited public details (not applicable here - all facilities are county-operated)
4. **IGSA Details:** County-level agreements may not be fully documented online
5. **Geographic Scope:** Investigation limited to I-4 corridor counties; adjacent counties not investigated
