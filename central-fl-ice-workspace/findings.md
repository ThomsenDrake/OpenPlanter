# ICE Detention Facilities Along I-4 Corridor, Florida
## Investigation Findings Report

**Report Date:** February 20, 2026  
**Geographic Scope:** Interstate 4 corridor, Tampa to Daytona Beach, Florida  
**Counties Covered:** Hillsborough, Pinellas, Polk, Orange, Seminole, Volusia

---

## Executive Summary

This investigation identified **9 ICE detention facilities** along Florida's I-4 corridor: **8 active facilities** and **1 planned facility**.

### Key Findings

- **Total Facilities:** 9
- **Active:** 8 county jails operating under Intergovernmental Service Agreements (IGSAs) with ICE
- **Planned:** 1 large-scale ICE Processing Center in east Orlando
- **Total Confirmed Capacity:** 11,326 beds across all facilities
- **ICE-Dedicated Capacity:** 1,630 beds (where specified)
- **Planned Capacity:** 1,500 beds at new Orlando facility

All active facilities participate in the **287(g) program**, which authorizes local law enforcement to perform immigration enforcement functions. Florida is unique in having **100% county jail participation** in 287(g) agreements statewide.

### Entity Resolution

Facilities were matched across sources using:
- Exact name matching
- Address verification (street, city, county, ZIP)
- ICE facility codes (where available)
- Cross-referencing news reports with official records

### Confidence Levels

Each facility was assigned a confidence level based on source quality:

- **Confirmed:** Multiple independent sources, including official government records
- **Probable:** Single reliable source with citations (news outlets, advocacy reports with documentation)
- **Possible:** Mentioned in sources but not independently verified
- **Unconfirmed:** Rumored or suggested without solid documentation

## Analysis of Findings

### Geographic Distribution

The I-4 corridor facilities are distributed as follows:
- **West (Tampa Bay):** 3 facilities (Hillsborough x2, Pinellas x1)
- **Central:** 3 facilities (Polk x1, Orange x1 active, Seminole x1)
- **East (Daytona Beach):** 2 facilities (Volusia x2)
- **Planned Central (Orlando):** 1 facility

### Capacity Analysis

**Total Confirmed Bed Capacity:** 11,326
- Hillsborough County: 5,011 beds (44%)
- Pinellas County: 3,271 beds (29%)
- Seminole County: 1,396 beds (12%)
- Orange County: 1,053 beds (9%)
- Volusia County: 595 beds (5%)

**ICE-Dedicated Capacity:** 1,630 beds (where specified)
- Orange County: 130 beds (66 men + 64 women cap)
- Orlando Planned: 1,500 beds

### Program Participation

All 8 active facilities participate in **287(g) programs**:
- 7 facilities: 287(g) basic
- 1 facility (Pinellas): 287(g) + Secure Communities
- 1 facility (Orlando planned): Dedicated ICE Processing Center

**Florida Unique Factor:** As of 2026, Florida is the only state with 100% county jail participation in 287(g) agreements, mandated by state law.

### Operational Trends

1. **Surge in Detentions:** Multiple facilities report significant increases in ICE detainees
   - Orange County: "More than fourfold" surge
   - Pinellas County: Detainees quadrupled over two years
   - Volusia County: 220 detainees since 2025

2. **Contract Disputes:** Orange County's IGSA is threatened with cancellation over reimbursement rates ($88/day deemed insufficient)

3. **Expansion Plans:** Major new ICE Processing Center planned for Orlando
   - Part of nationwide ICE expansion
   - 1,500-bed short-term processing facility
   - Would significantly increase ICE capacity in Central Florida

## Limitations and Gaps

### Data Limitations

1. **ICE Facility Codes:** Not available for all facilities (only Pinellas and Polk confirmed)
2. **Detainee Populations:** Exact ICE detainee counts not publicly disclosed for most facilities
3. **Contract Details:** IGSA start/end dates, renewal terms not publicly available for many agreements
4. **Capacity Ambiguity:** Reported capacities are total facility capacity, not ICE-dedicated beds (unless specified)
5. **Private Contractors:** No private contract facilities identified in I-4 corridor (all are county-operated IGSAs)

### Temporal Limitations

- **Report Date:** February 20, 2026
- **Data Currency:** Most information current as of February 2026
- **Planned Facility Status:** Orlando ICE Processing Center based on news reports; status may change
- **MOA Dates:** Most 287(g) agreements signed 2019-2025; operational details may have changed

### Geographic Limitations

- This report covers only facilities in direct I-4 corridor counties
- Nearby facilities outside corridor (Baker County, etc.) excluded
- Some detainees may be transferred to facilities outside corridor

### Transparency Gaps

- ICE does not publicly disclose real-time detainee populations
- County-level IGSA terms not always published online
- Planned facilities may not be officially announced until contracts signed
- Financial terms of 287(g) agreements not always transparent

## Conclusion

Florida's I-4 corridor hosts a substantial ICE detention infrastructure, with 8 active county jails holding ICE detainees through 287(g) Intergovernmental Service Agreements. The planned 1,500-bed Orlando ICE Processing Center represents a significant expansion that would create the first dedicated ICE facility in the corridor.

All facilities are operated by county sheriff's offices rather than private contractors. The region's ICE detention capacity is concentrated in the Tampa Bay area (Hillsborough and Pinellas counties), which together account for 73% of total bed capacity.

The investigation reveals active expansion of ICE detention operations along the I-4 corridor, with all counties participating in 287(g) programs and a major new processing center under consideration in Orlando. Current facilities report surging detainee populations, suggesting continued growth in detention operations throughout the region.
