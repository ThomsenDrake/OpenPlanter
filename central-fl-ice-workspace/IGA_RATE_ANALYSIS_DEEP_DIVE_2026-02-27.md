## Executive Summary

This analysis compares Intergovernmental Agreement (IGA) rates and terms across five county facilities in the I-4 corridor that house federal detainees for the U.S. Marshals Service (USMS). **Key finding: Per-diem rates vary by up to 152.7% ($61.06/day) between the highest and lowest counties, raising significant questions about pricing equity and cost allocation methodologies.**

## Key Findings & Questions for Investigation

### 1. **Extreme Rate Disparities**
- **152.7% difference** between highest and lowest per-diem rates
- **164.6% difference** in guard transportation rates
- No clear justification for such variance within the same federal district

### 2. **Osceola County Anomaly**
- **40-year-old rates** with no modifications
- $40/day is likely **significantly below actual costs**
- **Questions:**
  - Why has this rate never been updated?
  - Is Osceola County subsidizing federal detention?
  - Are there side agreements or supplemental payments?
  - Is this facility still operational under this IGA?

### 3. **Hillsborough County Pricing**
- **Highest per-diem rate** ($101.06) despite having guard rates 34% below Orange County
- **Overcharge history:** Modification 6 (1996) required rate reduction for overcharges
- **21 years without rate review** since 2005
- **Questions:**
  - Is current rate justified by actual costs?
  - Has there been another overcharge since 1996?
  - Why no rate adjustments since 2005?

### 4. **Orange County — Only Facility Authorized for ICE**
- **Unique authorization:** Orange County is explicitly authorized to house ICE detainees
- Other facilities have ICE box **unchecked** in agreements
- **Guard rate premium:** $31.75/hr is 52% higher than Hillsborough ($20.94)
- **Questions:**
  - Why is Orange the only ICE-authorized facility?
  - Does ICE pay different rates than USMS?
  - Are ICE detainees mixed with USMS detainees?

### 5. **Rate Update Mechanisms**
- Pinellas has explicit **36-month adjustment pathway** via DSNetwork
- Hillsborough went **21 years** without adjustment
- Osceola went **40 years** without adjustment
- **Questions:**
  - Why do some counties update rates regularly while others don't?
  - Is DSNetwork used consistently?
  - What triggers rate reviews?

### 6. **Capacity vs. Utilization**
- Pinellas: **400 beds** (largest capacity)
- Osceola: **32 beds** (estimated, smallest)
- **Questions:**
  - What are actual utilization rates?
  - Are higher rates correlated with lower utilization?
  - Do smaller facilities have higher per-detainee costs?

## Conclusion

This analysis reveals **significant pricing disparities** in federal detention agreements across the I-4 corridor. The 152.7% variance in per-diem rates between counties—without clear justification—suggests either:

1. **Inconsistent rate-setting practices** across the USMS district
2. **Outdated agreements** that don't reflect current costs (Osceola, Hillsborough)
3. **Potential overcharging** in some facilities (Hillsborough history)
4. **Subsidization** in others (Osceola's 40-year-old rate)

The **Osceola County anomaly**—operating on 1986 rates for 40 years—raises the most urgent questions and should be prioritized for investigation.
