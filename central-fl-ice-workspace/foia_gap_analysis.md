# FOIA Gap Analysis: ICE Detention Facilities - I-4 Corridor, Florida

**Analysis Date:** February 20, 2026  
**Objective:** Identify which investigation limitations can be bridged through FOIA (Freedom of Information Act) and Florida Sunshine Law requests

---

## Executive Summary

Of the **15 identified limitations** in the ICE detention facilities investigation, **12 limitations (80%)** can be partially or fully addressed through strategic FOIA and public records requests to federal and state agencies. This analysis provides a targeted approach to obtaining missing data through formal records requests.

### Key Findings:
- **HIGH FOIA POTENTIAL (9 limitations):** Can be directly addressed with specific FOIA requests
- **MODERATE FOIA POTENTIAL (3 limitations):** Can be partially addressed, may require multiple requests
- **LOW/NO FOIA POTENTIAL (3 limitations):** Either publicly unavailable by law or not FOIA-able

## Detailed Analysis by Limitation Category

### A. DATA LIMITATIONS

#### 1. ICE Facility Codes - MISSING FOR MOST FACILITIES
**Status:** ⚠️ **HIGH FOIA POTENTIAL**

**Current Gap:** Only 2 of 9 facilities (Pinellas, Polk) have confirmed ICE facility codes

**FOIA Strategy:**
- **Agency:** ICE Enforcement and Removal Operations (ERO)
- **Request Type:** Federal FOIA
- **Specific Request:** "All ICE facility identification codes (FICE codes) for facilities listed in the ICE detention facility directory as of [date], specifically including but not limited to facilities in Hillsborough, Pinellas, Polk, Orange, Seminole, and Volusia counties, Florida"
- **Expected Response Time:** 20-30 business days (may be extended)
- **Likelihood of Success:** HIGH - This is administrative data with minimal exemption claims

**Alternative Approach:**
- Request from DHS Privacy Office (parent agency)
- Request ICE Facility Management database extract

---

#### 2. Detainee Populations - NOT PUBLICLY DISCLOSED
**Status:** ⚠️ **HIGH FOIA POTENTIAL**

**Current Gap:** Exact ICE detainee counts not available for most facilities

**FOIA Strategy:**
- **Agency:** ICE Enforcement and Removal Operations
- **Request Type:** Federal FOIA
- **Specific Requests (Multiple Needed):**
  1. "Daily population reports for ICE detainees at the following facilities from January 1, 2024 to present: [list all 9 facilities]"
  2. "Monthly average daily population (ADP) statistics for ICE detainees at facilities in Florida's I-4 corridor counties"
  3. "Booking and release records for ICE detainees at [specific facility] for period [X to Y]"
  
- **Expected Response Time:** 30-60 business days (likely extended)
- **Likelihood of Success:** MODERATE-HIGH
  - ICE may claim privacy exemptions (Exemption 6/7(C)) for individual-level data
  - Aggregate statistics more likely to be released
  - Request " segregable" data (totals without personal identifiers)

**Alternative Sources:**
- Request from Office of Immigration Statistics (OIS) at DHS
- TRAC Immigration already publishes some population data (use as baseline)

---

#### 3. Contract Details (IGSA Terms) - NOT PUBLICLY AVAILABLE
**Status:** ⚠️ **HIGH FOIA POTENTIAL**

**Current Gap:** IGSA start/end dates, renewal terms, and specific contract language not available

**FOIA Strategy:**
- **Agency:** ICE Procurement Office + GSA (General Services Administration)
- **Request Type:** Federal FOIA
- **Specific Requests:**
  1. **ICE:** "Copies of all Intergovernmental Service Agreements (IGSAs) between ICE and the following county sheriff's offices: Hillsborough, Pinellas, Polk, Orange, Seminole, Volusia counties, Florida, including all amendments, modifications, and renewal terms"
  2. **GSA:** "All contract records, solicitation documents, and award notices for ICE detention services in the following Florida counties: [list]"
  3. **ICE:** "Per-bed reimbursement rates and payment schedules for each IGSA facility in Florida's I-4 corridor"

- **Expected Response Time:** 30-45 business days
- **Likelihood of Success:** HIGH for contract documents (these are public contracts)
  - May encounter Exemption 4 (commercial/financial information) claims
  - Counter-argument: These are public funds agreements with government entities
  - Request redacted versions if necessary

**Parallel State Law Requests:**
- **Agency:** County Sheriff's Offices (Hillsborough, Pinellas, Polk, Orange, Seminole, Volusia)
- **Request Type:** Florida Public Records Law (Chapter 119, Florida Statutes)
- **Specific Request:** "All IGSA contracts, amendments, and correspondence with ICE regarding detention services"
- **Advantage:** Faster response (10-30 days), fewer exemptions

---

#### 4. Capacity Ambiguity (ICE-Dedicated vs. Total Beds)
**Status:** ⚠️ **MODERATE FOIA POTENTIAL**

**Current Gap:** Reported capacities are total facility capacity, not ICE-dedicated beds

**FOIA Strategy:**
- **Agency:** ICE ERO + County Sheriff's Offices
- **Request Type:** Federal FOIA + Florida Public Records
- **Specific Requests:**
  1. **ICE:** "ICE-dedicated bed capacity allocations for each facility under IGSA in Florida's I-4 corridor counties, including any capacity adjustments or reallocations from 2020 to present"
  2. **County Sheriffs:** "Bed allocation records showing ICE-dedicated beds vs. general inmate population beds at [facility name]"
  3. **ICE:** "Any capacity modification requests or approvals for I-4 corridor facilities"

- **Likelihood of Success:** MODERATE
  - ICE may claim operational security concerns
  - County records more accessible under state law
  - Look for "bed space agreements" or "capacity utilization reports"

2. **Pinellas County Sheriff's Office**
   - IGSA contracts, ICE capacity allocation, reimbursement records

   - 287(g) program records, ICE detainee counts, funding records

## FOIA Request Templates

### Template 1: IGSA Contract Request (Federal)
```
Pursuant to the Freedom of Information Act, 5 U.S.C. § 552, I request:

All Intergovernmental Service Agreements (IGSAs) between ICE and [County Name] 
Sheriff's Office for detention services, including:
- Original agreement and effective date
- All amendments and modifications
- Renewal terms and conditions
- Per-diem reimbursement rates
- Capacity allocations (ICE-dedicated beds)
- Performance metrics and reporting requirements
- Termination clauses

Please include all related correspondence regarding contract negotiations, 
modifications, or disputes from January 1, 2020 to present.

This request is for non-commercial, educational and journalistic purposes. 
I request a waiver of all fees under 5 U.S.C. § 552(a)(4)(A)(iii). If fees 
cannot be waived, please contact me before processing if fees will exceed $50.
```

---

### Template 2: Detention Population Data (Federal)
```
Pursuant to FOIA, I request:

Daily and monthly detention population statistics for ICE detainees at the 
following facilities from January 1, 2023 to present:

1. Hillsborough County Orient Road Jail (Tampa, FL)
2. Hillsborough County Falkenburg Road Jail (Tampa, FL)
3. Pinellas County Jail (Clearwater, FL)
4. Polk County Jail (Bartow, FL)
5. Orange County Jail (Orlando, FL)
6. John E. Polk Correctional Facility (Sanford, FL)
7. Volusia County Branch Jail (Daytona Beach, FL)
8. Volusia County Correctional Facility (Daytona Beach, FL)

Please provide:
- Average daily population (ADP)
- Monthly booking counts
- Monthly release/transfer counts
- Peak population dates and counts

If individual-level data cannot be released pursuant to Exemption 6 or 7(C), 
please provide aggregate statistical summaries that do not contain personally

```

### Template 3: Florida Public Records Request (County Level)
```
Pursuant to Article I, Section 24 of the Florida Constitution and Chapter 119, 
Florida Statutes, I request the following public records:

All contracts, agreements, and memoranda of understanding between [County Name] 
Sheriff's Office and U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) regarding 
detention services, including:

1. Intergovernmental Service Agreements (IGSAs)
2. 287(g) program Memoranda of Agreement
3. Reimbursement rate schedules
4. ICE detainee capacity allocations
5. Financial records showing ICE payments to the county
6. Correspondence with ICE regarding contract terms or disputes

Please include all amendments, modifications, and related correspondence from 
January 1, 2020 to present.

Pursuant to Section 119.07(1)(a), Florida Statutes, if you determine any portion 
of the requested records is exempt from disclosure, please provide a written 
citation to the specific statutory exemption and provide the non-exempt portions 
of the records.

This request is for journalistic and public interest purposes. Please contact me 
regarding any estimated fees before processing.
```

## Anticipated Exemptions and Counter-Arguments

### Exemption 4: Commercial/Financial Information
**Claim:** Contract terms contain proprietary pricing or business information  
**Counter:** IGSA agreements are between government entities using public funds; not commercial information  
**Strategy:** Request redacted versions if pricing claimed exempt

---

### Exemption 5: Pre-Decisional/Deliberative Process
**Claim:** Orlando facility planning is pre-decisional  
**Counter:** Request segregable factual information (site visits, capacity assessments)  
**Strategy:** Request "factual portions" and market research (not deliberative analysis)

---

### Exemption 6/7(C): Personal Privacy
**Claim:** Individual detainee records protected  
**Counter:** Request aggregate statistical data without personal identifiers  
**Strategy:** Frame request for "statistical summaries" and "aggregate data"

---

### Exemption 7(E): Law Enforcement Techniques
**Claim:** Capacity allocations reveal security vulnerabilities  
**Counter:** Total capacity already public; ICE-specific allocation is administrative data  
**Strategy:** Emphasize operational capacity is distinct from security procedures
