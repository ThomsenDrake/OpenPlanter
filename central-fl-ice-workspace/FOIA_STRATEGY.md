## Objective
Close high-impact evidence gaps in ICE I-4 corridor analysis through parallel Florida public records requests and federal FOIA submissions.

## Overall Assessment
12 of 15 known limitations are addressable through records requests.

## Tier 1 Priorities (Submit/Track First)
1. ICE detention statistics data dictionary and methodology (OIS).
2. IGSA/IGA contracts, amendments, reimbursement terms (county + ICE procurement).
3. ICE facility code mapping for I-4 facilities (ICE ERO).
4. 287(g) operational and financial records (ICE 287(g) office + county sheriffs).
5. ODO inspection and compliance reports (2020-2026).

## Tier 2 Priorities
- Historical aggregate population series.
- Orlando facility procurement and planning documents.
- Transfer pattern summaries.
- ICE-dedicated bed allocation records.

## Tier 3 / Lower-Yield Items
- Real-time population status (FOIA unsuitable).
- Adjacent county expansion requests (defer unless scope expands).
- Pre-procurement planning assumptions without official records.

## Execution Model
### Phase 1: Florida state/local records
- Submit to Hillsborough, Pinellas, Polk, Orange, Seminole, Volusia.
- Request contract terms, reimbursement records, capacity allocations, and 287(g) records.

### Phase 2: Federal FOIA
- ICE ERO, ICE ODO, ICE 287(g), ICE Procurement, DHS Privacy Office, GSA PBS, OIS.

### Phase 3: Appeals and escalation
- File administrative appeals for denials or over-redactions.
- Convert high-value unresolved denials into litigation candidates.

## Expected Outcomes
### Minimum
- Core contract set for counties in scope.
- Facility code normalization.
- At least partial inspection and aggregate population disclosures.

### Optimal
- Full contract/amendment history.
- Transfer and capacity pattern clarity.
- Orlando procurement chain and timeline evidence.
- Clean reconciliation for all major unanswered questions.
