### Legal Analysis

**5. 287(g) Legal Requirements (Community Justice Project)**
- **File:** `287g (1).pdf`
- **Author:** Community Justice Project (updated May 2, 2025)
- **Key Findings:**
  
  **NOT Required for:**
  - City police agencies
  - State law enforcement
  - University/college police
  - K-12 school police
  - Sheriffs who DON'T operate jails
  
  **Required ONLY for Jail Operators:**
  - Must sign A 287(g) agreement - but can CHOOSE model
  - Three models: Task Force (broadest), Jail Enforcement, WSO (narrowest)
  - Florida law: "No requirement to participate in particular program model"
  - **FACT:** All 67 counties signed Task Force Model on Feb 26, 2025
  - This was NOT required - could have chosen WSO only
  
  **Termination Process:**
  - Agreements are indefinite but terminable
  - Written notice to local ICE field office
  - No penalty for withdrawal
  
  **Why Beyond Requirements:**
  - Fear of Governor DeSantis suspension threats
  - "Preemptive compliance" strategy
  - Political pressure, not legal requirement

### Financial Analysis

**6. BOA Basic Information (Southern Poverty Law Center)**
- **File:** `BOA Basic Info 2019.pdf`
- **Author:** Southern Poverty Law Center (Summer 2019)
- **Key Facts:**
  
  **What BOAs Are:**
  - Created by Sheriff Gualtieri (Pinellas) as "constitutional workaround"
  - ICE pays $50 per immigrant (up to 48 hours)
  - Effective rate: **$25/day** for 2-day hold
  - Compare to: Orange County's **$88/day** IGSA rate
  
  **Florida BOA Counties (29 as of Feb 2019):**
  - Pinellas, Lee, Manatee, Bay, Walton, Hernando, Brevard, Polk, Indian River, Charlotte, Monroe, Sarasota, Columbia, Santa Rosa, Suwannee, Hillsborough, Pasco, De Soto, Flagler, Highlands, Martin, Nassau, Okeechobee, Palm Beach, Seminole, St. Johns, St. Lucie, Lake
  - Expanded to Louisiana: St. Charles Parish
  
  **Legal Issues:**
  - SPLC/ACLU lawsuit (Dec 2018): Monroe County held U.S. citizen
  - Constitutional concerns: 48-hour holds without judicial warrant
  - Sheriff Gualtieri admitted concerns (April 2017 email)
  
  **How BOAs Work:**
  - Not a contract - an "understanding of how ICE and counties will do business"
  - Each "order" for an immigrant is separate contract
  - ICE "orders" immigrants like supplies
  - Counties "supply and deliver" them

### 2. Financial Dynamics Revealed

| Rate Type | Amount | Context |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Orange County IGSA** | $88/day | Current reimbursement |
| **Orange County Actual Cost** | $180/day | County calculation |

### 3. 287(g) Over-Implementation

**Legal Requirements vs. Reality:**

| Agency Type | Legal Requirement | Actual Practice |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Jail Operators | YES - any model | All 67 signed Task Force (highest) |
| City Police | NO | Many signed voluntarily |
| University Police | NO | Some signed voluntarily |
| School Police | NO | Absent from law |
| Sheriffs (no jail) | NO | Some signed voluntarily |

**Key Finding:** All 67 counties could have chosen WSO (narrowest) but instead chose Task Force (broadest) - likely due to DeSantis threats, not legal requirement.

### 4. Rebooking Practice Details

**How It Worked:**
1. ICE detainee held for 72 hours
2. Remove from jail
3. Rebook under NEW booking number
4. 72-hour clock resets
5. Repeat indefinitely

**Problems Created:**
- Prolonged detention without due process
- Attorneys couldn't locate clients
- System updates lagged behind rebookings
- Resource strain on jail operations

**Orange County Response:**
- Demings: "Circumvents intended operation of IGSA"
- **ENDED March 1, 2026**

**Outstanding Question:** Is practice continuing in other counties?
