### 1. Orange County IGSA Crisis - Detailed Timeline

**March 25, 2025:** Board of County Commissioners ratifies WSO 287(g) program
- Orange County correctional officers now serve immigration arrest warrants
- **ONLY applies to individuals already in custody** (cannot make community arrests)
- 17 officers trained and credentialed as Warrant Service Officers
- WSO is the narrowest 287(g) model - legally required, hardest to eliminate

**February 26, 2025:** All 67 Florida counties operating jails entered 287(g) agreements simultaneously
- **NOT REQUIRED** for most agencies - preemptive compliance due to DeSantis threats
- Many signed Task Force Model (highest authority) when only WSO required for jail operators
- Sheriff Gualtieri (Pinellas) admitted constitutional concerns about detainers in 2017

**April 2025:** ICE arrestees surge begins in Orange County
- 5,000+ ICE immigration inmates booked over 8 months (April-December 2025)
- ICE exceeded IGSA daily estimate of 94 men and 20 women

**August 11, 2025:** Orange County requests IGSA renegotiation
- Current reimbursement: **$88/day**
- Actual county cost: **$180/day**
- Shortfall: **$92/day per detainee**

**December 22, 2025:** Demings Letter #1 to USMS
- County has received 3,626 additional ICE inmates since August 11
- Total deficit: **$333,592+** in unreimbursed costs
- Demings: "Fiscal impact unfairly shouldered by Orange County taxpayers"

- **CAPS detainees without charges:** 66 men + 64 women maximum
- **ENDS REBOOKING PRACTICE:** No more rebooking to circumvent 72-hour hold limit
- Current stats (Feb 2026): 191 inmates with ICE hold + local charges; 96 held without charges
- Previous stats (2 weeks earlier): 200+ with hold + charges; 158 without charges

**February 13, 2026:** Demings Letter #2 - **MARCH 13 DEADLINE**
- Sets March 13, 2026 deadline for IGSA renegotiation
- Threatens IGSA termination if fair reimbursement not reached
- USMS has "not offered a reimbursement rate reasonable to our requests"

**March 1, 2026:** Caps and rebooking ban take effect

- Rebooking practice officially ended

**March 13, 2026:** **IGSA RENEGOTIATION DEADLINE (8 DAYS AWAY)**

### 2. Rebooking Practice - How ICE Circumvented Legal Limits

**The Practice:**
- ICE would remove detainees every 72 hours, rebook under new booking number
- Artificially reset the 72-hour clock
- Prolonged detention without due process
- Attorneys couldn't locate clients - system updates lagged behind rebookings

**Legal Issue:**
- Detainees should only be held 72 hours without charges
- Rebooking circumvented this limit
- Created confusion for legal representation

**Orange County Response:**
- Demings: Practice "strains OCCD resources, disrupts workflow, circumvents IGSA operation"
- **ENDED as of March 1, 2026**

### 3. BOA (Basic Ordering Agreement) - The Financial Alternative

**What BOAs Are:**
- Agreements for counties WITHOUT IGSA
- ICE pays **$50 per immigrant** (up to 48 hours detention)
- Effective rate: **$25/day** for 2-day holds
- Compared to: Orange County's $88/day IGSA rate

**BOA Counties in Florida (29 as of Feb 2019):**
- Pinellas, Lee, Manatee, Bay, Walton, Hernando, Brevard, Polk, Indian River, Charlotte, Monroe, Sarasota, Columbia, Santa Rosa, Suwannee, Hillsborough, Pasco, De Soto, Flagler, Highlands, Martin, Nassau, Okeechobee, Palm Beach, Seminole, St. Johns, St. Lucie, Lake
- Expanded to Louisiana: St. Charles Parish

**BOA Legal Issues:**
- SPLC/ACLU lawsuit (Dec 2018) - Monroe County held U.S. citizen
- Constitutional concerns about 48-hour holds without judicial warrant
- Sheriff Gualtieri admitted concerns about detainer constitutionality (April 2017)

**Orange County's BOA Threat:**
- County presentation (July 2025) framed BOA as consequence of IGSA termination
- **ANALYSIS SHOWS THIS IS MISLEADING:**
  - BOAs are NOT required by law
  - Voluntary decision with much lower reimbursement ($25/day vs $88/day)
  - If Orange County terminates IGSA, they could refuse BOA
  - Real motive: County wants maximum reimbursement, not compliance

### 4. 287(g) Legal Requirements - What's Actually Required

**NOT Required:**
- City police departments
- State law enforcement agencies
- University/college police
- K-12 school police
- Sheriffs who DON'T operate jails

**Required ONLY for Jail Operators:**
- Must sign **A** 287(g) agreement - but can choose model
- Three models available: Task Force (broadest), Jail Enforcement, WSO (narrowest)
- Florida law: "No requirement to participate in particular program model"
- **FACT:** On Feb 26, 2025, ALL 67 counties signed Task Force Model (highest authority)
- This was NOT required - could have chosen WSO only

**Termination Process:**
- Current 287(g) models are **indefinite** - remain until one party ends it
- Local agencies can terminate "at any time, in writing to local ICE field office"

**Why Agencies Signed Beyond Requirements:**
- Fear of Governor DeSantis suspension threats
- "Preemptive compliance" strategy
- Not legal requirement but political pressure
