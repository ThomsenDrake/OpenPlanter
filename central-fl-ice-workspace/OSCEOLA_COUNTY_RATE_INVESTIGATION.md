# Osceola County Rate Investigation: Why Hasn't the Rate Been Updated in 40 Years?

**Investigation Date:** February 27, 2026  
**Status:** ACTIVE INVESTIGATION  
**Priority:** CRITICAL 🚨

---

## Executive Summary

Osceola County Sheriff's Department has maintained a **$40.00/day** per-diem rate for federal detainees since **September 1, 1986** - making it the oldest unchanged rate in the I-4 corridor and possibly the entire USMS system.

**Critical Discovery:** A modification in 1993 briefly increased the rate to $52.00/day, but the current rate appears to have reverted to the original $40.00/day, suggesting possible administrative errors, reversed modifications, or dormant agreement status.

### 2. **Rate Adjustment Mechanism Exists But Never Used** 📋

**IGA Article V permits annual rate adjustments:**

> "The rate may be renegotiated not more than once a year, after agreement has been in effect for twelve months."

**Process requires COUNTY initiative:**
1. Local Government submits written request to U.S. Marshal (60 days prior)
2. Must include completed Cost and Pricing Data Sheet
3. Must provide additional cost information
4. Must permit audit of accounting records
5. Criteria: federal cost standards (OMB circulars)
6. **Unless other justification, increases limited to National Inflation Rate**

**40 years = 40 missed opportunities for adjustment**

## Why Hasn't Osceola County Updated Its Rate?

### Hypothesis 1: **Dormant/Inactive Agreement** 🔒
- Facility may no longer house federal detainees regularly
- Agreement kept active for emergency overflow capacity
- No incentive to update rates for rarely-used facility
- **Evidence:** Not in current ICE data, very small capacity (32 beds)

### Hypothesis 2: **Administrative Neglect** 📝
- County never submitted rate adjustment requests
- Lack of awareness of Article V adjustment process
- Turnover in sheriff's office/administration
- No dedicated federal detention coordinator
- **Evidence:** 40 years of no adjustments (or reversal of 1993 increase)

### Hypothesis 3: **Side Agreements/Compensatory Arrangements** 💵
- Informal agreements not captured in IGA documents
- Federal grants or equipment provided in lieu of rate increases
- USMS-provided property or training offsetting costs
- **Evidence:** Article VII allows USMS to furnish excess federal property

### Hypothesis 4: **Political/Strategic Reasons** 🏛️
- County deliberately subsidizing federal detention
- Political statement or policy choice
- Avoiding perception of "profiting" from incarceration
- **Evidence:** None found yet

### Hypothesis 5: **Cost Structure Anomaly** 📊
- Facility has exceptionally low operating costs
- Shared infrastructure with county jail reduces marginal costs
- Minimal dedicated staff/equipment for federal detainees
- **Evidence:** Article II requires "same level of medical care as local prisoners"

### Hypothesis 6: **1993 Modification Reversal** 🔄
- Rate increase to $52.00 in 1993 was later reversed
- Possible overcharge recoupment (like Hillsborough 1996)
- Agreement renegotiated back to original terms
- **Evidence:** Current rate is $40.00, not $52.00

## What Would Trigger a Rate Review?

### Article V Requirements:

1. **County initiates:** Written request 60 days prior
2. **Documentation:** Completed Cost and Pricing Data Sheet
3. **Audit access:** County must permit accounting records audit
4. **Federal standards:** Must meet OMB cost standards
5. **Inflation cap:** Unless justified, limited to national inflation rate
6. **USMS approval:** Contracting Officer must sign modification

### Alternative Triggers:

1. **USMS audit:** Unscheduled facility inspection (Article IX)
2. **Complaint/investigation:** Detainee or advocacy group complaint
3. **Congressional inquiry:** Oversight committee request
4. **Media exposure:** Investigative reporting on rate disparities
5. **Competitive pressure:** New facility opening (Orlando Processing Center)

## Conclusion

Osceola County's 40-year-old $40.00/day rate is an extreme outlier that suggests either:

1. **Administrative neglect** - The county has never initiated a rate adjustment
2. **Dormant agreement** - The facility rarely houses federal detainees
3. **Hidden arrangements** - Side agreements compensate for low rate
4. **Reversed modification** - The 1993 increase was later reversed

The 1993 modification mystery is critical - understanding why the rate increased to $52.00 and then reverted to $40.00 could explain the entire 40-year stagnation.

**Most likely scenario:** The facility is dormant or minimally utilized for federal detention, and there has been no incentive for either party to update the rate. However, this leaves the agreement technically active, potentially creating liability if utilization increases.

**Recommendation:** Immediate FOIA requests to resolve the 1993 modification discrepancy and confirm current operational status.
