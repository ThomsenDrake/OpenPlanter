# Executive Summary: Trump Administration ICE Operations - I-4 Corridor

**Quick Reference Guide | February 27, 2026**

---

## What Current ICE Operations Look Like Under Trump

### Federal Level (Jan 2025 - Feb 2026)
- **Funding:** $75 billion through 2029 (+837% increase)
- **National Population:** 70,000 detainees (up from <40,000)
- **287(g) Agreements:** 1,427 nationwide (957% increase from Dec 2024)
- **Private Contractors:** CoreCivic + GEO Group = $2B+ each in 2025 revenue

### Florida Level
- **State Population:** 5,231 detainees (Feb 2026), **+278% growth** from March 2024
- **Asylum Backlog:** 178,000 cases (#1 nationally)
- **Immigration Courts:** 38 judges (Miami: 24, Orlando: 14)

### I-4 Corridor Structure
**Total: 9 facilities across 6 counties**

**Tier 1: Active Detention (3 facilities, 303 detainees)**
- Hillsborough Orient Road Jail (132 detainees) - **NEW in Oct 2025**
- Orange County Jail (~100 detainees)
- Pinellas County Jail (4 detainees)

**Tier 2: 287(g) Book-and-Release (5 facilities, 7,000+ beds)**
- Hillsborough Falkenburg Road
- Polk County Jail
- John E. Polk Correctional (Seminole)
- Volusia County Branch Jail
- Volusia County Correctional
- **Status:** Have immigration enforcement authority but NOT in detention stats
- **Hypothesis:** Arrest → Identify → Transfer to Tier 1 for detention

**Tier 3: Private Contractor (1 facility, 167 detainees)**
- Orlando ICE Processing Center
- **CRITICAL ANOMALY:** 167 detainees but NOT in official ICE facility list
- **Ownership:** Unknown (likely CoreCivic or GEO Group)

## Other Notable Weak Points

### Osceola County: 40-Year Rate Stagnation ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Rate unchanged since 1986: $40/day
- Estimated county subsidy: **$65+ million over 40 years**
- Leverage: Fiscal irresponsibility, demand rate renegotiation

### Book-and-Release Transfer Dependencies ⭐⭐⭐
- 5 Tier 2 facilities (7,000+ beds) depend on Tier 1 for detention
- Blocking transfers reduces effective capacity
- Leverage: FOIA for transfer patterns, target Tier 1 intake refusal

### USMS Pass-Through Routing ⭐⭐⭐
- Counties contract with USMS, not ICE directly
- Reduces transparency (ICE not direct contractor)
- Leverage: Expose routing as accountability avoidance
