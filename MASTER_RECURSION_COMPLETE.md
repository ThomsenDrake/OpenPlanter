## Finding 2: BOA Stealth Network ⭐ (Score: 92/100)

**29 Florida counties signed Basic Ordering Agreements (BOAs) with ICE in FY2018**, creating a standardized $25/day detention framework that functions as latent surge capacity.

### What BOAs Enable
- ICE can request bed space from any BOA county **without negotiating a new agreement**
- $25/day is a standardized national rate — counties cannot negotiate
- No volume commitments or minimums — beds activated on demand
- BOA counties can be activated with minimal public notice

### The 29-County Pool
Bay, Brevard, Charlotte, Columbia, DeSoto, Flagler, Hernando, Highlands, **Hillsborough**, Indian River, Lake, Lee, Manatee, Martin, Monroe, Nassau, Okaloosa, **Orange**, **Osceola**, **Palm Beach**, Pasco, **Pinellas**, **Polk**, **Sarasota**, **Seminole**, St. Johns, St. Lucie, **Volusia**, **Walton**

*Bold = also has IGSA/IGA or 287(g) — dual-agreement counties*

### BOA Number Sequence (70CDCR18G series)
| Number | County | Source | Confidence |
|--------|--------|--------|------------|
| #15 | Sarasota County Sheriff | MuckRock FOIA (opclaudia) | CONFIRMED |
| #16 | Walton County Sheriff | MuckRock FOIA (opclaudia) | CONFIRMED |
| #01–#14 | Unknown | — | Unmapped |
| #17–#29 | Unknown | — | Unmapped |

**Only 2 of 29 BOA numbers mapped (6.9%).** The numbering is NOT alphabetical (Sarasota = alphabetical #24, not #15). The consecutive assignment of #15/#16 to Sarasota/Walton suggests batch processing order or signing date sequence.

### Why This Matters
The dual-agreement structure (BOA + IGSA in the same county) creates a two-tier system:
- **IGSA** = higher rate ($56.71–$88/day), negotiated, county has leverage
- **BOA** = $25/day standard, no negotiation, ICE holds all leverage
- If a county terminates its IGSA (as Orange County may on March 13), ICE can potentially fall back to the BOA at $25/day — **reducing county revenue per detainee by 72%**

**Source:** PSL-Resources "BOA Basic Info 2019.pdf", MuckRock FOIA documents  
**Confidence:** HIGH for pool membership, MEDIUM-HIGH for dual-agreement implications
