## What Current ICE Operations Look Like Under Trump

### The Big Picture

The Trump administration (starting January 2025) has transformed ICE operations through:

1. **$75 billion federal funding surge** (through 2029) - an 837% increase
2. **957% increase in 287(g) agreements** nationwide (from ~135 to 1,427)
3. **Removal of location protections** (schools, churches, hospitals now targeted)
4. **No-bid contracts** to private prison companies (CoreCivic, GEO Group)

### Florida Impact

- **Statewide detention population:** 5,231 detainees (Feb 2026), up from 1,385 in March 2024 (**+278% growth**)
- **Asylum case backlog:** 178,000 cases - #1 nationally
- **Immigration courts:** 38 judges (Miami: 24, Orlando: 14)

## Other Notable Weak Points

### Hillsborough Implementation Delays ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- 287(g) signed Feb 26, 2025 → First detainees Oct 2025 = **7-month delay**
- Future expansions face same bottlenecks
- Falkenburg Road Jail: 3,300 beds UNUSED, IGSA pending
- **Pressure:** County Commission approval required, organize opposition

### Book-and-Release Transfer Dependencies ⭐⭐⭐
- 5 Tier 2 facilities (7,000+ beds) depend on Tier 1 for detention
- Blocking transfers reduces effective capacity
- **Pressure:** FOIA for transfer patterns, target Tier 1 intake refusal

### USMS Pass-Through Routing ⭐⭐⭐
- Orange and Pinellas contract with USMS, not ICE directly
- Counties can claim "we don't contract with ICE" - political cover
- **Pressure:** Expose routing as accountability avoidance

### Osceola County 40-Year Rate Stagnation ⭐⭐⭐
- Rate: $40/day (unchanged since 1986)
- Estimated county subsidy: **$65+ million over 40 years**
- **Pressure:** Fiscal irresponsibility, demand rate renegotiation
