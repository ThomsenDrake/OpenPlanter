LEGAL REQUIREMENTS UNDER FLORIDA LAW:

1. Chapter 119.07(1)(a), Florida Statutes, requires that "Every person who has 
   custody of a public record shall permit the record to be inspected and copied 
   by any person desiring to do so, at any reasonable time, under reasonable 
   conditions, and under supervision by the custodian of the public records or 
   the custodian's designee."

2. The custodian must respond within a "reasonable time." Florida courts and 
   the Attorney General have consistently held that:
   - An acknowledgment of receipt should be provided within a few business days
   - If the request cannot be fulfilled immediately, the agency must provide 
     an estimated timeline
   - Complete silence for nearly two weeks is NOT reasonable

3. Chapter 119.07(1)(b), Florida Statutes, requires the custodian to "state the 
   estimated cost of providing copies" and whether the records are "exempt or 
   confidential." No such communication has been provided.

4. The Florida Supreme Court has held that failure to respond to a public records 
   request can constitute a violation of law subject to enforcement action. 
   See Wait v. Florida Power & Light Co., 372 So. 2d 420 (Fla. 1979).

MY ORIGINAL REQUEST SOUGHT:

A comprehensive set of public records related to the Scout microtransit program, 
including:
- Complete RFP 604918-25/PCD and all vendor proposals
- Fully executed contract with BeFree LLC and all amendments
- BCC meeting minutes, recordings, and presentations (June 2024-present)
- Financial records including invoices, payments, and budget documents
- Performance data including ridership, response times, and utilization rates
- Planning documents, feasibility studies, and cost-benefit analyses
- Communications between county staff and BeFree LLC representatives

This request is a matter of significant public interest, involving substantial 
taxpayer expenditure (up to $9.2 million annually) for a new transit program that 
replaced an established public bus system. The public has a right to understand 
how this program was planned, procured, and implemented.
