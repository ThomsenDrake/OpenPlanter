## What to Expect

### Timeline

**Under Florida Law:**
- Agency must respond within a "reasonable time"
- Usually 5-10 business days for initial acknowledgment
- Complex requests may take 30+ days
- You can request interim responses with partial records

### Possible Responses

1. **Full Grant:** All requested records provided
2. **Partial Grant:** Some records provided, others exempt
3. **Denial:** Must cite specific statutory exemption
4. **Request for Clarification:** Agency needs more specificity
5. **Cost Estimate:** If fees will be charged

### Fees (if any)

**Typical Costs:**
- First 30 minutes of search time: FREE
- Search/retrieval: ~$15-20/hour after first 30 minutes
- Copying: $0.15 per page for paper copies
- Electronic copies: Usually minimal or free if already digitized
- Redaction review time: ~$20-30/hour

**Fee Waiver:** The request includes a fee waiver request based on public interest. If denied, you'll receive an estimate before work begins.

## Legal Basis for Your Request

### Florida Statutes

**Chapter 119, Florida Statutes - Public Records:**
- Provides right of access to public records
- Covers all state, county, and municipal records
- Very broad definition of "public records"
- Limited exemptions must be specifically cited

**Article I, Section 24, Florida Constitution:**
- Constitutional right of access
- Presumption of openness
- Exemptions must be narrowly construed

### Key Legal Points

1. **Presumption of Openness:** All records are public unless specifically exempted
2. **Burden on Agency:** Agency must prove record is exempt, not vice versa
3. **No "Why" Required:** You don't have to explain why you want the records
4. **No Commercial Restriction:** Records can be used for any purpose
5. **Right to Inspect:** Can view records before copying
6. **Right to Copy:** Can photograph, scan, or copy records

## Records Most Likely to be Exempt or Redacted

Based on typical public records exemptions:

1. **Trade Secrets:** Vendor proprietary information in proposals (partial)
2. **Personal Information:** Home addresses, phone numbers of private citizens
3. **Security Information:** Specific operational security measures
4. **Attorney-Client Privilege:** Some legal memos (rare)
5. **Ongoing Litigation:** Records related to active lawsuits

**Note:** Even if portions are exempt, the non-exempt portions must be provided with redactions.
