## Pushback Strategy

### 1. Challenge the MicroTransit Manager Hours (Primary Target)

**Why 18 hours is unreasonable:**
- This suggests the manager will spend **nearly 2.5 full business days** reviewing records
- For a typical PRR, an administrator reviews documents for redaction decisions, not line-by-line reading
- Administrative review should be proportionate to the volume and sensitivity of records

**Questions to ask:**
1. "What specific tasks require the MicroTransit Division Manager's direct involvement for 18 hours?"
2. "Can you break down how these 18 hours will be allocated (review, redaction, consultation)?"
3. "Is the manager personally reviewing every document, or making policy decisions about redactions?"
4. "Could a lower-cost staff member perform initial document sorting before manager review?"

**Requested reduction:** Cut to **4-6 hours maximum** (savings: ~$940-$1,100)

---

### 2. Challenge the Director-Level Rates

Both the Director of Budget ($122.50/hr for 3 hours) and Director of Communications ($124.52/hr for 5 hours) are charging premium administrative rates.

**Florida law (Chapter 119) requires fees be based on actual costs, not inflated rates.**

**Questions to ask:**
1. "Do these rates reflect the actual hourly wage of these positions, or an administrative markup?"
2. "Can you provide the hourly wage basis for these rates per Florida's cost calculation requirements?"
3. "Are these directors personally reviewing documents, or can staff-level employees handle this?"

**Requested reduction:** Challenge the 5 hours for Communications Director - cut to **2 hours** (savings: ~$375)

---

### 3. Request Itemization for Clerical Time

The clerical staff (Purchasing, PRR Coordinator) total 10 hours at ~$55-61/hour.

**Questions to ask:**
1. "Can you provide a breakdown of what specific clerical tasks require 5 hours each for Purchasing and PRR coordination?"
2. "What volume of documents are being processed?"
3. "Are these estimates based on actual time tracking or projected estimates?"

---

### 4. Cite Florida Public Records Law

**Florida Statute §119.07(4)** states that fees must be:
- Based on the **actual cost** of providing the service
- Reasonable and directly attributable to the request
- Agencies cannot charge for review time unless it requires extensive clerical or supervisory assistance

**Sample language for your response:**

> "Under Florida Statute §119.07, agencies may only charge for the actual cost of extensive use of resources. The estimate provided includes 18 hours of administrative time for the MicroTransit Division Manager, representing 44% of the total cost. Please provide:
> 
> 1. A detailed breakdown of tasks requiring 18 hours of manager-level review
> 2. Documentation of the hourly wage basis for all administrative rates charged
> 3. An explanation of why lower-cost staff cannot perform initial document processing
> 
> I am willing to pay reasonable costs but request that administrative review time be reduced to 4-6 hours, with staff-level employees handling document preparation. This would bring the estimate to approximately $2,000-$2,200, which I believe more accurately reflects the actual cost of fulfilling this request."
