# ICE Detention Population Trends: I-4 Corridor Facilities
**Analysis Date:** February 27, 2026
**Data Period:** FY2020-FY2026 (through Feb 12, 2026)

---

## 📊 Executive Summary

Analysis of ICE detention statistics reveals **steady expansion** of ICE detention along Florida's I-4 corridor from FY2020 to FY2026:

- **FY2020:** No I-4 corridor facilities in ICE detention statistics
- **FY2021:** 1 facility (Pinellas)
- **FY2022:** 2 facilities (Pinellas + Orange)
- **FY2023:** 1 facility (Pinellas only - Orange gap)
- **FY2024:** 2 facilities (Pinellas + Orange returns)
- **FY2025:** 2 facilities (continuation)
- **FY2026:** 3 facilities (Pinellas + Orange + **Hillsborough NEW**)

**Key Finding:** Growth from 0 to 3 facilities over 6 years, with a notable gap year for Orange County in FY2023.

**Timeline Analysis:**
- **Feb 26, 2025:** 287(g) agreement signed

- **Gap:** ~7 months between 287(g) signing and ICE detention start

**Critical Question:** Why did Hillsborough take 7 months after 287(g) signing to begin holding ICE detainees?

**Hypothesis:**
- IGSA contract negotiation took time (Hillsborough is IGSA, not USMS IGA)
- Facility preparation/modification required
- ICE capacity planning and allocation process
- Different operational model than Pinellas/Orange

## 🏢 Facility Type Analysis

### USMS IGA vs. IGSA Pattern

| Facility | Type | Start Year | Pattern |
|----------|------|------------|---------|
| Pinellas | USMS IGA | FY2021 | Consistent presence |
| Orange | USMS IGA | FY2022 | Gap year FY2023 |
| Hillsborough | IGSA | FY2026 | NEW |

**Key Difference:**
- **USMS IGA** (Pinellas, Orange): Joint-use with US Marshals Service - holds USMS prisoners + ICE detainees + county inmates
- **IGSA** (Hillsborough): Direct ICE agreement - different operational and funding model

**Impact:** Hillsborough's IGSA status may explain why it took longer to begin ICE detention after 287(g) signing.
