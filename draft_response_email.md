The current invoice lists only generic categories ("Excessive Administrative Time" / "Excessive Clerical Time") with no explanation of the work to be performed.

1. **MicroTransit Division Manager – 18 hours ($1,411.20):** What tasks require 18 hours — more than two full workdays — of this individual's time? Is this time for searching records, reviewing for exempt content, or something else?

2. **Director of Budget – 3 hours ($367.50):** What tasks require a Director-level employee at $122.50/hour?

3. **Director of Communications – 5 hours ($622.60):** What tasks require a Director-level employee at $124.52/hour?

These three "Excessive Administrative Time" line items total **$2,401.30 — approximately 75% of the total labor charges**. I need to understand what work justifies these costs.

## Concerns Regarding Legal Compliance

Section 119.07(1)(b), Florida Statutes, authorizes a special service charge only when the nature or volume of records requires **"extensive clerical or supervisory assistance,"** and provides that the charge **"shall be reasonable"** and based on **"the labor cost of the personnel providing the service that is actually incurred by the agency."**

I have the following concerns:

**1. Use of Director and Manager-Level Staff for Tasks That Appear Clerical in Nature**

The statute specifically references "clerical or supervisory assistance." Searching email databases, compiling responsive records, and copying documents are clerical functions that do not require Director-level personnel at rates of $78–$125 per hour. If the County elects to assign high-salaried administrators to perform work that lower-paid clerical staff could do, it is not reasonable to pass that inflated cost to the requester. The charge should reflect the rate of the lowest-paid employee capable of performing the work.

**2. Exemption Review Is the Agency's Legal Obligation**

If any of the estimated time is for reviewing records to determine whether statutory exemptions apply, please be aware that this is an obligation the agency bears under Chapter 119 regardless of any public records request. The cost of fulfilling the agency's own legal duty to identify exempt records should not be charged to the requester.

**3. Reasonableness of 18 Hours for a Single Employee**

Eighteen hours for the MicroTransit Division Manager is the single largest charge on this invoice and accounts for nearly half of the total labor cost. I respectfully request a specific explanation of what tasks require this volume of time from this individual.

## Proposal to Reduce Costs

In the interest of resolving this efficiently, I propose the following:

### Phase 1: Readily Available Records (Minimal or No Special Service Charge)

Many of the records in my original request should be readily accessible without extensive search effort:

- The RFP 604918-25/PCD (standard procurement file)
- All vendor proposals received in response to the RFP
- The executed contract with BeFree LLC and all amendments
- BCC meeting agendas, minutes, presentations, and recordings related to the Scout program
- Budget documents and financial records (invoices, payments) for the Scout program
- Planning documents and feasibility studies

These are discrete, already-compiled records. Their production should not require 39 hours of staff time across seven employees, and I request that these be produced promptly at the statutory per-page copy rate or at no cost in electronic format.

### Phase 2: Email Searches (Negotiate Scope)

I understand that the email search component (Item 7) drives the bulk of the estimated cost. I am willing to discuss narrowing the scope of the email searches to reduce the County's production burden, including:

- Reducing the number of search terms
- Narrowing date ranges
- Limiting the accounts searched to specific named individuals
- Prioritizing certain searches and deferring others

I would welcome a conversation about which adjustments would most significantly reduce the estimated cost.

## Additional Requests

3. **Publicly available records:** If any of the requested records are already posted on the County's website or public portals (e.g., BCC meeting minutes, RFP documents, budget documents), please identify them so I can access them directly and exclude them from this request.
