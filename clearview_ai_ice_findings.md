# Clearview AI Records — Orange County Sheriff's Office (OCSO)
## ICE/Immigration Investigation Relevance Analysis

**Source:** `PRR_25-6990_Clearview_AI.xlsx` — Public Records Request response from OCSO  
**Records:** 6,007 Clearview AI facial recognition searches (Oct 2021 – Aug 2025)  
**Analysis Date:** March 2026

---

## Executive Summary

The OCSO Clearview AI search log contains **direct evidence of OCSO personnel conducting facial recognition searches for federal immigration enforcement purposes**, as well as broader DHS/ICE collaboration through HSI (Homeland Security Investigations) task force operations. Key findings:

1. **One search explicitly categorized as "Immigration"** using an HSI case number format (May 2025)
2. **25 searches tied to HSI case numbers** spanning 2022–2025, with 82+ individual facial recognition queries
3. **At least 4 smuggling/human trafficking cases** where Clearview AI was used
4. **6 searches tied to CFIX** (Central Florida Intelligence Exchange, a DHS-funded fusion center)
5. **46% of all searches (2,777) were conducted by personnel whose identities are shielded** under Florida surveillance and undercover exemptions, making it impossible to determine how many additional searches may have been immigration-related

- **2,777 searches (46.2%)** have both creator name and email redacted under Florida statutory exemptions.
- The **undercover personnel exemption** (72 records) is particularly significant — it is used for officers whose identities must be protected because of covert assignments, consistent with task force officer (TFO) deployments to federal agencies like HSI.
- Because nearly half of all searches are unattributable, the true scale of immigration enforcement use of Clearview AI by OCSO **cannot be determined from this dataset alone**. The single "Immigration" categorization may represent only the cases that were labeled as such.

## Cross-References to Existing Investigation

This dataset connects to the broader ICE investigation in several ways:

1. **OCSO-ICE Operational Nexus**: The Clearview AI records provide the first direct evidence of OCSO personnel conducting facial recognition searches for HSI/ICE investigations, including one explicitly categorized as immigration enforcement. This supplements existing evidence of OCSO's IGSA detention agreements and BCC resolutions regarding ICE cooperation.

2. **287(g) / Task Force Officer Context**: The pattern of exempt-creator HSI case numbers is consistent with OCSO deputies assigned as Task Force Officers to HSI. This is a different cooperation mechanism than 287(g) — TFOs are deputized by the federal agency rather than operating under a 287(g) agreement.

3. **Surveillance Technology in Immigration Enforcement**: Clearview AI's facial recognition technology is being deployed in immigration cases. This raises questions about whether the technology's procurement and use policies at OCSO contemplated immigration enforcement applications, and whether there are any county-level restrictions on such use.

4. **CFIX Fusion Center**: The Central Florida Intelligence Exchange serves as an intelligence-sharing node connecting OCSO, HSI, and other federal agencies. CFIX intelligence tasks and briefings appear in the Clearview AI search log, indicating facial recognition is being used as part of fusion center intelligence operations.

## Methodology

### Data Source
- **File**: `PRR_25-6990_Clearview_AI.xlsx` (Public Records Request 25-6990)
- **Agency**: Orange County Sheriff's Department
- **Format**: Single Excel sheet ("PRR Clearview Searches"), 6,007 data rows, 10 columns
- **Coverage**: October 18, 2021 – August 27, 2025

### Analysis Approach
1. Full-text keyword search across all fields for immigration-related terms: `ice`, `immigra`, `detainer`, `287(g)`, `alien`, `deport`, `removal`, `customs`, `ero`, `cbp`, `border`, `federal`, `dhs`, `homeland`
2. Pattern matching for federal case number formats (HSI `OL__` prefix, FBI `281__` prefix, etc.)
3. Crime type classification analysis for immigration-adjacent categories
4. Creator/exemption type analysis to identify task force activity patterns
5. Temporal analysis of search volumes and federal case activity

### Known Limitations
- **46% of records are unattributable** due to Florida statutory exemptions — the true scope of immigration-related searches is likely understated.
- **Crime type classification is user-entered** — searches for immigration purposes may be categorized under other types (e.g., "Other," "Fugitive warrant").
- **The dataset contains search metadata only** — no information about search subjects, photos, or match results is included.
- **HSI case number interpretation** is based on known format conventions; specific group/classification codes have not been independently verified against HSI's internal coding scheme.
- **No "Success" field data** for most records — only 2 records have Success values populated, limiting assessment of Clearview AI's operational impact.
