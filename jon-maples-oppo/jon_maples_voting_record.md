## Executive Summary

Jon Maples served as Council Member Group 2 on the Lake Clarke Shores Town Council from April 11, 2023, until his resignation on October 31, 2025. During his tenure, he also served as Vice Mayor (2024-early 2025) and Chair of the Zoning Board of Adjustment.

### Key Findings:

1. **Voting Pattern:** Overwhelmingly voted with council consensus - the vast majority of votes were unanimous (5-0 or 4-0 when members absent)
2. **Dissenting Votes:** Only ONE dissenting vote documented during entire tenure
3. **Recusals:** ONE recusal due to conflict of interest
4. **Leadership Role:** Served as Vice Mayor during 2024 and early 2025, frequently making and seconding motions
5. **Mayoral Aspiration:** Was nominated for Mayor position in April 2025 but did not receive the appointment
6. **Recognition:** Named "Home Rule Hero" by Florida League of Cities (2025)

## Notable Votes and Positions

### 1. Dissenting Vote - Resolution 2023-07 (April 11, 2023)
**Issue:** State Revolving Fund Loan Program authorization  
**Maples Vote:** NO (Vote passed 4-1)  
**Reasoning:** Maples expressed concerns about:
- Interest rates on loans
- Debt burden on residents who cannot afford payments
- Whether alternative funding sources had been explored

**Significance:** This was his FIRST major vote as a council member and his ONLY documented dissenting vote during his entire tenure.

### 2. Recusal - Resolution 2023-06 (April 11, 2023)
**Issue:** Resolution expressing appreciation for Jon Maples's service on Zoning Board of Adjustment  
**Maples Action:** RECUSED from discussion and vote  
**Reason:** Conflict of interest - vote was about himself  
**Vote Outcome:** Approved 4-0

### 3. Edgewater Park Annexation - Resolution 2023-32 (October 10, 2023)
**Issue:** Annexation of Edgewater Park enclave  
**Maples Role:** **Seconded the motion**  
**Vote:** Approved 5-0  
**Context:** Controversial issue with significant resident opposition; Maples actively supported the annexation

### 4. Water/Sewer Rate Increase - Resolution 2024-26 (November 12, 2024)
**Issue:** 2.9% increase in water and sewer service rates for FY 2024/2025  
**Maples Role:** **Made the motion** as Vice Mayor  
**Significance:** As Vice Mayor, Maples took lead on rate increase affecting all residents

### 5. Budget Transfer Authority - Resolution 2024-24 (October 8, 2024)
**Issue:** Authorization for Town Manager to transfer budget amounts between departments  
**Maples Role:** **Made the motion** as Vice Mayor  
**Significance:** Administrative flexibility measure

## Conclusion

Jon Maples's voting record as Lake Clarke Shores Town Council member demonstrates:

1. **Consensus-Oriented:** Voted with council majority in virtually all cases (only one dissenting vote in 2.5 years)
2. **Fiscally Cautious:** Early concern about debt burden (dissenting vote on loan program)

His resignation to pursue higher office follows standard Florida practice for local officials seeking state positions.
