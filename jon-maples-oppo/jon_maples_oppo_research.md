## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Jon Maples, 43, is a financial advisor and former Lake Clarke Shores Town Council member running for Florida House District 87 in the March 24, 2026 special election. He previously lost a 2020 race for Palm Beach County Commission by a 60-40 margin. Maples has aligned himself closely with Donald Trump's "America First" agenda and has received Trump's endorsement. Key vulnerabilities include his extreme positions on guns (supports lowering the gun purchase age from 21 to 18), opposition to vaccine mandates, close ties to the insurance industry while running on insurance reform, and his previous electoral defeat. His opponent, Emily Gregory, is a small business owner and public health professional who has been endorsed by the Democratic Legislative Campaign Committee as a "spotlight race."

## VI. POLICY POSITIONS & VULNERABILITIES

### Guns (HIGH VULNERABILITY)
**Position:** Supports lowering gun purchase age from 21 to 18

**From Sun Sentinel Questionnaire:**
> "I support protecting the Second Amendment rights of law-abiding adults."

**Attack Angle:** This reverses post-Parkland reforms and puts him at odds with majority of Florida voters who supported the age increase after the Marjory Stoneman Douglas shooting.

### Vaccine Mandates (HIGH VULNERABILITY)
**Position:** Opposes all vaccine mandates

**From Sun Sentinel Questionnaire:**
> "I support parental rights. We should not let government stand between a parent and doctor making the best health care decision for their child."

**Attack Angle:** Aligns with DeSantis/Ladapo anti-vaccine positions; could be framed as anti-science and endangering public health.

### Property Insurance (MODERATE VULNERABILITY)
**Position:** Acknowledges insurance company profits, wants them "shared with consumers"

**From Sun Sentinel Questionnaire:**
> "The state delivered insurance reforms a few years ago. There are indications that these are addressing the business climate that insurers said were so problematic and providers are enjoying record profits. Those profits need to be shared with consumers in the form of rate reductions, and the legislature needs to hold the providers feet to the fire in that regard."

**Attack Angle:** 
- Received $5,000 from Florida Insurance Council PAC
- His "solution" is weak - just asking insurers to voluntarily reduce rates
- No concrete plan to force rate reductions
- Conflict of interest given his insurance industry employment and donations

### Property Taxes (MODERATE VULNERABILITY)
**Position:** Open to eliminating homestead property taxes

**From Sun Sentinel Questionnaire:**
> "I support giving Floridians meaningful tax relief. I'm open to shifting some of the tax burden to our booming tourism industry..."

**Attack Angle:** Property tax elimination is fiscally irresponsible and would devastate local services and schools.

### Government Transparency (POTENTIAL ASSET)
**Position:** Supports transparency even in university presidential searches

> "I believe in transparency and accountability — even in state university presidential searches."

**Note:** This is a mainstream position that may not be an attack point.

### Climate Change (WEAK POSITION)
**Position:** "Home-hardening" and "resiliency measures"

**Attack Angle:** Fails to address root causes; focuses only on individual property owner responsibility rather than systemic action.

## XII. CONCLUSION

Jon Maples presents multiple attack surfaces for a general election campaign. His extreme positions on guns and vaccines, his previous electoral defeat, his insurance industry conflicts, and his close alignment with Trump's MAGA movement all provide contrast opportunities with Emily Gregory's mainstream Democratic positions on healthcare, public safety, and working families.

The district's demographics - wealthy but with affordability concerns, older voters, high education levels - suggest that kitchen-table issues (insurance costs, property taxes, healthcare) will resonate more than culture war issues. Maples's weak policy proposals and industry ties undermine his credibility on these core issues.

**Recommended Strategy:** Focus on (1) guns and public safety, (2) healthcare and science, (3) insurance industry conflicts, and (4) his previous rejection by voters. Frame Gregory as the competent, mainstream choice versus Maples's extremism and special interest ties.
