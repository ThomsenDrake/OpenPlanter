## VI. REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

### A. Lobbying Disclosure Act (LDA)

Under the Lobbying Disclosure Act of 1995, individuals must register as lobbyists if they:

1. Make more than one lobbying contact
2. Spend more than 20% of their time on lobbying activities for a client
3. Receive income for lobbying activities

**Jon Maples's Status:** Based on the absence of his name in LD-2 filings, Maples likely:
- Does not meet the 20% time threshold for lobbying activities
- Engages primarily in grassroots advocacy (mobilizing others rather than direct lobbying)
- Participates in educational/political activities that don't require registration

### B. Federal Election Campaign Act (FECA)

NMPAC operates under FECA regulations as a corporate-connected PAC:
- Can accept contributions only from Northwestern Mutual's "restricted class" (executives, administrative personnel, and stockholders)
- Must report all contributions and disbursements to the FEC
- Limited to $5,000 per candidate per election
