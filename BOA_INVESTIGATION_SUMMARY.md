## Legal Analysis Summary

### Federal Authority: BOAs are Voluntary

**Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) 16.703:**
- BOAs are "written instruments of understanding," NOT contracts
- Government has NO obligation to place orders under a BOA
- Service providers are NOT required to accept orders
- BOAs cannot imply any agreement to place future orders

**National Sheriffs' Association:**
> "The Government is under no obligation to issue orders on a BOA, and a service provider that enters into a BOA is not required to accept any order."

### Constitutional Authority: Anti-Commandeering

**Printz v. United States (1997):**
- Supreme Court held federal government CANNOT compel state/local officials to administer federal programs
- Anti-commandeering doctrine protects local sovereignty
- Counties retain "inviolable sovereignty" over law enforcement

**Justice Scalia (Majority Opinion):**
> "The power of the Federal Government would be augmented immeasurably if it were able to impress into its service—and at no cost to itself—the police officers of the 50 States."

### State Law: Cooperation, Not Participation

**Florida SB 168 (2019):**

**Requires:**
- ✓ Honoring 48-hour ICE detainers
- ✓ Notifying ICE of release dates
- ✓ Providing inmate information
- ✓ Allowing ICE jail access

**Does NOT Require:**
- ✗ Signing BOAs
- ✗ Signing IGSAs
- ✗ Housing detainees beyond 48 hours
- ✗ Accepting federal detention contracts

## Orange County Analysis

### The Claim

**Orange County officials have said:** If the county terminates its IGSA with the U.S. Marshals Service, it will be **forced** to sign a BOA with ICE.

**This claim is LEGALLY FALSE.**

### The Reality

**What County CAN Do:**
1. Terminate IGSA (deadline extended to March 31, 2026)
2. Refuse BOA (100% legal and voluntary)
3. Limit detention to 48-hour detainers only (SB 168 minimum)
4. Stop housing ICE detainees entirely (beyond 48 hours)

**What County MUST Do:**
1. Honor 48-hour detainers (SB 168 requirement)
2. Notify ICE of releases
3. Provide inmate information
4. Allow ICE jail access

### Financial Truth

| Scenario | Federal Revenue | County Cost | Net Impact |
|----------|----------------|-------------|------------|
| **Current IGSA** | $88/day | $180/day | **-$92 per detainee** |
| **BOA (if chosen)** | $25/day | $180/day | **-$155 per detainee** |
| **No IGSA, No BOA** | $0 | $0 | **$0 (no long-term detention)** |

**Key Insight:** Orange County's "financial crisis" is a **CHOICE**, not a mandate. The county voluntarily loses $92/day by choosing to house detainees beyond the 48-hour detainer period.

### Political Analysis

**County's Motive for False Claim:**
1. **Negotiating leverage** with USMS for better IGSA rates
2. **Avoid political blame** for choosing to house detainees at a loss
3. **Claim "no choice"** to deflect criticism from coalition partners
4. **Pressure advocates** to accept status quo

**The Truth:** County Commissioners have multiple legal options. They are choosing financial losses over ending detention cooperation.
